Title: Freeloading Cousin
Description: A struggling young man's act of kindness leads to an unexpected opportunity that could change his fortune forever.

INT. ROAST DUCK SHOP - DAY

COUSIN (20s, lean, quick-eyed) sweeps duck debris. His stomach RUMBLES. He glances at a GOLDEN CRISPY DUCK hanging by the counter.

DUCK SELLER (40s, weary) bags a duck for a CUSTOMER. The Customer pays with worn bills.

COUSIN'S eyes track the transaction. As the Customer turns, a piece of duck skin slides from the bag. In one fluid motion, Cousin catches it, pops it in his mouth.

DUCK SELLER
(catching him)
Hey! What're you doing?

COUSIN
(chewing, casual)
Cousin, just checking it's not spoiled.

DUCK SELLER
(snatching the broom)
Spoiled my ass. You work three days, you eat for free. Those are the rules.

COUSIN
(grinning)
Guess I'm still on day two, then.

EXT. COUSIN'S HOUSE ENTRANCE - DAY

Rain drizzles. GRANDPA (80s, frail but sharp) clutches a faded PHOTO of young Cousin with his parents.

OLDER COUSIN (30s, well-fed) and his WIFE hustle Grandpa toward a waiting car.

OLDER COUSIN
We're late. Let's go.

Grandpa turns back. Cousin stands in the doorway, holding a WARM BOILED EGG.

COUSIN
Grandpa. For the road.

Grandpa takes it. Their hands touch. The rain falls harder between them. Grandpa's mouth trembles, but no words come. He nods once, slow and heavy.

The car door SLAMS. Tail lights vanish into the gray downpour.

EXT. WAREHOUSE - DAY

Cousin hauls fifty-pound rice sacks. Sweat darkens his camo shirt. He stacks them with machine precision.

BOSS (50s, gruff) counts cash, then holds out THREE RED BILLS.

BOSS
Five days. No complaints.

Cousin takes the money. Counts it. Folds it carefully into his shoe.

COUSIN
Thank you, boss.

BOSS
(shrugging)
You earned it. Come back Monday.

EXT. OLD HOUSE - DAY

Cousin trudges up to a sagging wooden house. Paint peels like old skin. He stops on the steps, exhausted.

QIAN CAIQING (20s, beautiful despite threadbare clothes) sits on a stool, dabbing her lips with crushed petals for color. She spots him and smiles.

QIAN CAIQING
Tough day?

COUSIN
Forces a smile. Pulls out the three bills.

COUSIN
Got us something.

He produces a small BUNDLE: second-hand clothes. She unfolds a dress. The SEAM splits slightly.

QIAN CAIQING
(laughing despite herself)
Champion of the discount bins.

COUSIN
(defensive)
The vendor said it was last year's style—

QIAN CAIQING
(pressing finger to his lips)
Hush. It's perfect.

She holds it against her body, twirls. For a moment, they both forget the roof leaks.

COUSIN
Your mother called again?

QIAN CAIQING
(sighs)
She wants twenty thousand dowry. Or we wait another year.

EXT. STREET - DAY

Cousin walks the crowded street in his warehouse camo. Billboards advertise LUXURY CARS, MANSIONS. He looks at his cracked phone screen: 86 RMB balance.

A siren WAILS in the distance.

EXT. ROAST DUCK SHOP - DAY

MR. WANG (60s, elegant but unassuming) guides MRS. WANG (50s, pregnant, radiant) toward the shop.

MR. WANG
The skin is crispier than the city hotels.

MRS. WANG
Then what are we waiting—

A SCREECH of tires. A DELIVERY VAN swerves toward them. Mr. Wang freezes.

Cousin, passing by, SHOVES them aside. The van's MIRROR clips his shoulder. He hits the ground hard.

Mrs. Wang screams. Blood pools beneath her.

MR. WANG
(frantic)
Someone call an ambulance!

COUSIN
(clutching his arm)
Hospital's two blocks. My tricycle's faster.

He points to a battered THREE-WHEELER with a cargo bed.

EXT. HOSPITAL - CONTINUOUS

Cousin pedals wildly through traffic, Mr. Wang cradling Mrs. Wang in back.

MR. WANG
(holding her bleeding head)
Stay with me. Please stay.

INT. HOSPITAL EMERGENCY ROOM - DAY

DOCTOR (40s, calm, urgent) and NURSES wheel Mrs. Wang away. Mr. Wang tries to follow. A NURSE stops him.

NURSE
Wait here.

MR. WANG
(to Cousin, trembling)
You saved us. Thank you.

COUSIN
(grimacing at his own cut)
She'll be okay. Strong lady.

MR. WANG
How can I ever—

COUSIN
Just doing what's right.

The Doctor emerges, pulling off bloody gloves.

DOCTOR
Mr. Wang? Your wife has severe internal bleeding. She needs AB negative. We have... one unit. We need three.

MR. WANG
I'll pay anything. Import it.

DOCTOR
No time. The nearest is two hours away.

COUSIN
(quietly)
I'm AB negative.

MR. WANG
(turning to him)
What?

COUSIN
Test me. If it matches, take it.

INT. HOSPITAL - BLOOD DRAW ROOM - DAY

Cousin lies on a bed, tube in his arm. Blood flows dark red into a bag. He watches it, pale.

Mr. Wang sits beside him, holds his other hand.

MR. WANG
I have no children. This baby was... our hope. Now I just want my wife.

COUSIN
You'll have both.

MR. WANG
Why are you doing this?

COUSIN
Someone once told me: when you can give, you give. That's all.

INT. HOSPITAL CORRIDOR - LATER

Cousin stumbles out, arm bandaged. Mr. Wang supports him.

COUSIN
(weak smile)
You owe me a watermelon. The expensive kind.

MR. WANG
(laughs through tears)
I'll buy you the whole farm.

The Doctor reappears.

DOCTOR
She's stable. Your blood saved her.

MR. WANG
(to Cousin)
My name is Wang Dekuang. My home is empty. Too empty. If you ever need...

INT. HOSPITAL LOBBY - LATER

BUTLER LIN (50s, precise) arrives with TWO BODYGUARDS. He takes in Cousin's bloodied camo, the bandage.

BUTLER LIN
Mr. Wang? Is Madam—

MR. WANG
Stable. Thanks to this young man. Where's the village project file?

BUTLER LIN
(pulling out a TABLET)
Ready. But sir, the board—

MR. WANG
(to Cousin)
You understand hard work. You understand sacrifice. Our rural education initiative needs someone who's lived it. Not another MBA.

COUSIN
(confused)
Me? I barely finished high school.

MR. WANG
You finished life school. Top of your class. Try it. Three months. Paid.

BUTLER LIN
(softly, to Mr. Wang)
Sir, his background check—

MR. WANG
I know exactly who he is. The only person who mattered when my world was ending.

Butler Lin hesitates, then bows to Cousin.

BUTLER LIN
Young Master Wang. Welcome to Kuangshi Group.

COUSIN
(overwhelmed)
I just wanted the watermelon.

MR. WANG
(chuckling)
You'll get it. And maybe a future worth building.
