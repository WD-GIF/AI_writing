Title: Fortune Baby's Destiny
Description: A young girl cursed with bad luck must leave her temple home to find her destined family and break the curse that affects all around her.

INT. DILAPIDATED TEMPLE - DAY

A dusty sunbeam cuts through the temple. FU BAO (8) sits on a cushion, perfectly still.

MASTER SHAN YUAN (O.S.)
Fu Bao.

FU BAO opens her eyes.

FU BAO
Master?

MASTER SHAN YUAN (60s), robes frayed at the edges, hurries in clutching a bowl of rice.

MASTER SHAN YUAN
Look. Actual alms.

He offers it. A silver ingot drops in. They watch it harden into stone. Neither reacts—it's routine.

Master sighs, then produces a steamed bun from his sleeve.

MASTER SHAN YUAN
Plan B.

He turns toward the altar. His foot catches the threshold. He stumbles. The bun arcs through the air, petrifying before it lands.

FU BAO
Master!

She catches his elbow. For a moment, she just holds him steady.

FU BAO
(quietly)
I'll mend the threshold.

MASTER SHAN YUAN
The threshold is fine.

He straightens with a wince.

FU BAO (V.O.)
Three brothers gone. Two winters without worshippers. Master says the temple follows the seasons, but I know which season I brought.

MASTER SHAN YUAN
What did I teach you about cause and effect?

FU BAO
That they're tangled like kites.

MASTER SHAN YUAN
Find where your string leads. It untangles everything.

A roof tile SHATTERS beside them. This, too, is routine.

But then Master flexes his fingers. The tips crumble to dust.

FU BAO
(staring)
Master—

MASTER SHAN YUAN
The mountain is done with me, Little Fortune. It needs you to leave.

FU BAO
I can try harder. I'll—

MASTER SHAN YUAN
Then I'll be dust by winter. Is that what you want?

Fu Bao's lip trembles. She shakes her head.

MASTER SHAN YUAN
(softening)
Find the family that shares your name. They'll be the ones who need you most.

He draws three yellow talismans. Presses them into her palm.

MASTER SHAN YUAN
One for true danger. That's all I can anchor from here.

FU BAO
What if I can't find them?

MASTER SHAN YUAN
What if you can?

He turns her toward the gate.

Fu Bao hesitates, then kneels. Three quick kowtows. She doesn't look back as she walks out.

Master Shan Yuan watches her go, his hand still crumbling.


# === 剧本后半 ===

EXT. MOUNTAIN PATH - DAY

The HE family bounces in a donkey cart. WU CHUNHUA (40s) counts coins. LI XIUE (50s) sits rigid. HE DA JIANG (30s) drives. HE DA SHAN (28) lounges.

WU CHUNHUA
Six hundred wen. For the risk.

LI XIUE
We agreed on four hundred.

WU CHUNHUA
That was before your pig died. Bad luck has a price.

HE DA JIANG
(quietly)
The pig was sick.

WU CHUNHUA
The village calls it cursed. My cart, my rules.

HE DA SHAN
Just pay her, Big Brother. It's not like fortune favors you.

LI XIUE
A hundred wen for the return. Not one more.

WU CHUNHUA
Ha! Your eldest dead, your second sick—who else would take your grain? Who else would take you?

LI XIUE
You go too far!

HE DA JIANG
(firmly)
Enough.

The donkey STUMBLES. The cart jerks to a halt.

HE DA SHAN
Watch the road!

HE DA JIANG
(staring ahead)
There's a child.

FU BAO stands in the path, robes too big, smile too bright. Like she's holding something back.

HE DA SHAN
Perfect. More bad luck.

Silence.

FU BAO
Excuse me. Are you the He family?

Li Xiue's breath catches. He Da Jiang's knuckles whiten on the reins.

FU BAO
I think I'm supposed to come with you.

A long beat. The wind moves through the trees.

WU CHUNHUA
(coldly amused)
A hundred wen and you can have the whole lot of them. They're good at finding strays.

Fu Bao's gaze never leaves Li Xiue's face. Searching.

HE DA JIANG
(to his wife, low)
We can't even feed our own.

LI XIUE
(considering Fu Bao)
We've lost enough.

She doesn't reach for Fu Bao. But she doesn't turn away.

FU BAO
Thank you.

She climbs into the cart. The axle CREAKS. The donkey brays again, but Wu Chunhua flicks the reins, and they roll forward.

Fu Bao sits very still among them, holding her breath. Then she looks up and meets Li Xiue's eyes.

Her smile is real this time. And desperate. And hopeful. All at once.
