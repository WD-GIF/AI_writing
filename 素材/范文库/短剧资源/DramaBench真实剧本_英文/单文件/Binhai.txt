# === 剧本前半 (Fountain 格式) ===

Title: Binhai
Description: A down-on-his-luck man faces rejection, betrayal, and loss on his birthday, leading to a fateful encounter with a fox spirit who offers him a chance to change his destiny.

INT. COMPANY LOBBY - DAY

A dozen JOB SEEKERS crowd the modern lobby, resumes in hand. ZHANG YE (30s, worn suit) sits apart, staring at his reflection in a window—practicing a smile that doesn't reach his eyes.

The HR MANAGER emerges, flanked by SECURITY.

HR MANAGER
Thank you all for your time. We've made our selections. Those not called, please see yourselves out.

Dispirited, the crowd shuffles toward the exit. Zhang Ye stays rooted, gripping his folder.

ZHANG YE
Sir, I've been waiting five hours. Could you just—five minutes?

HR MANAGER
( already walking away )
Decisions are final.

ZHANG YE
My mother—she's sick. I need this job.

The Manager pauses, then gestures to Security without looking back.

Zhang Ye is gently but firmly escorted out.

EXT. COMPANY ENTRANCE - DAY

The guards deposit Zhang Ye on the sidewalk. His resume folder spills, papers scattering.

SECURITY GUARD
Some doors just don't open, brother. Don't take it personal.

The guards retreat inside. Zhang Ye kneels, gathering papers as a light rain begins.

His PHONE BUZZES. "Mom" flashes on screen. He stares at it, lets it ring twice before answering—voice steady, practiced.

ZHANG YE
Hey, Mom.

ZHANG YE'S MOTHER (O.S.)
How did it go?

ZHANG YE
( forcing brightness )
Really well. They said they'd call this week.

ZHANG YE'S MOTHER (O.S.)
That's wonderful. You come home early, hear me? I made your favorite—stuffed lotus root. We'll celebrate properly.

ZHANG YE
(choking on the word)
...Yeah.

INT. ZHANG YE'S APARTMENT - DAY

Small, cluttered. LI QIAN (late 20s) lounges on the sofa, phone pressed to her ear, laughing—an intimate, familiar laugh.

LI QIAN
...he's always at those pathetic interviews. You know I can't remember the last time he actually—

She freezes. ZHANG YE'S MOTHER stands in the doorway, grocery bags slipping from her hands. A carton of eggs SMASHES on the floor.

ZHANG YE'S MOTHER
Qianqian... who were you talking to?

LI QIAN
(recovers, defensive)
You scared me! Why are you sneaking around?

ZHANG YE'S MOTHER
I heard you. On the phone. Who is 'he'?

Li Qian stands, smoothing her dress. A beat of silence. Then:

LI QIAN
Don't pretend you don't know. This life—this apartment, your medical bills—it's all on me now. Your son can't even keep a job for six months.

ZHANG YE'S MOTHER
(quietly)
He's trying.

LI QIAN
Trying? I see his bank statements. The interviews. The rejections. I'm drowning here, and you're both—just—sinking me faster.

She moves to leave. The older woman touches her arm.

ZHANG YE'S MOTHER
Please. We can—

Li Qian yanks free. The mother stumbles, catches herself on the counter.

LI QIAN
(cold)
I'm done being ashamed. I want my life back.

She grabs her purse and exits, slamming the door.


# === 剧本后半 ===

INT. ZHANG YE'S APARTMENT - LATER

Zhang Ye enters. The apartment is dim. Li Qian sits at the kitchen table, a document waiting beside her packed suitcase.

ZHANG YE
Mom called. She's staying with Auntie tonight—what's going on?

LI QIAN
She fell. I didn't touch her. But while we're being honest...

She slides the document forward. "DIVORCE AGREEMENT."

ZHANG YE
You can't be serious.

LI QIAN
My lawyer drew it up. No contest, no alimony. Clean break.

ZHANG YE
(unfolding)
Where will you go?

LI QIAN
(stands)
Somewhere I don't have to pretend anymore.

Clipped FOOTSTEPS echo from the hallway. A man's silhouette passes the door window. Li Qian's eyes follow it, just for a second—but Zhang Ye catches it.

ZHANG YE
(low)
Him?

LI QIAN
At least he doesn't apologize for what he wants.

She leaves. Zhang Ye stares at the closed door. His gaze falls to a GIFT BOX on the counter—small, wrapped in newspaper.

He opens it: a modest cake, and a card in his mother's trembling hand.

ZHANG YE'S MOTHER (V.O.)
"Son—happy birthday. I'm proud of you, even when you're not. Eat something sweet. Love, Mom."

Zhang Ye touches the cake frosting with one finger. It comes away red. His hands are shaking.

EXT. BINHAI LAKE - NIGHT

Mist rises off black water. Zhang Ye sits on the bank, surrounded by empty bottles. He doesn't drink—just stares at his reflection.

ZHANG YE
(to himself)
Thirty-two years. Same face. Same story.

A small WHITE FOX emerges from the reeds, watching him with amber eyes. It approaches, tentative.

ZHANG YE
Hey, little guy. You hungry too?

He breaks off a corner of the cake from his pocket. The fox takes it delicately.

ZHANG YE
Yeah. Me too.

He stands, walks to the water's edge. The fox follows, whining softly. Zhang Ye looks down at the dark surface.

ZHANG YE
(whispers)
Just... tired.

He takes another step. The water touches his shoes.

The fox BARKS—once, sharp. Light blooms from it, fierce and silver. Zhang Ye shields his eyes. When he lowers his hand, a WOMAN stands there in white, fox-fire dancing around her fingertips.

FOX SPIRIT
You fed me. That debts a thread.

ZHANG YE
(stumbling back)
What—?

FOX SPIRIT
One wish, mortal. But threads cut both ways. The price... will be part of you.

ZHANG YE
(desperate, laughing)
Rich. I want to be rich. Powerful. So no one can—

FOX SPIRIT
(interrupting)
So you can become the one who looks away?

She smiles, knowing. The fox-fire flickers. Zhang Ye's bravado crumbles.

ZHANG YE
(quiet)
I want... I want to matter.

The Fox Spirit steps closer. Her fingers brush his chest.

FOX SPIRIT
Then we have a bargain.

FADE OUT.
