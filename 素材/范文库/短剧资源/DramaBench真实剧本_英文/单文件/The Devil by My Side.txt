# === 剧本前半 (Fountain 格式) ===

Title: The Devil by My Side
Description: A woman marries an older man for a dowry, only to endure abuse, betrayal, and ultimately a deadly conspiracy orchestrated by her husband and her best friend.

INT. WEDDING BANQUET PRIVATE ROOM - DAY

HE RUOQING (20s) wears a red toast dress, serving tea to WANG MOTHER (50s). Her husband WANG CHANGLIN (40s) watches with detached amusement. Both sets of parents are present.

WANG MOTHER (accepting tea)
A daughter-in-law is family. You'll serve well.

He Ruoqing's smile doesn't reach her eyes.

INT. WEDDING BANQUET HALL - DAY

Wang Changlin's phone buzzes. He glances at the screen—ZHANG MEIZHEN's name visible—then at He Ruoqing, who looks away.

WANG CHANGLIN
(to He Ruoqing)
Business call.

He pockets the phone, screen-side in.

INT. WANG FAMILY HOUSE - DAY

He Ruoqing scrubs dishes. Her hands are raw. Wang Mother watches, unimpressed.

WANG MOTHER
My son married down. Remember your place.

Wang Changlin enters, smelling of alcohol. He grabs He Ruoqing's arm.

WANG CHANGLIN
Where's the money I gave you?

HE RUOQING
For groceries—

WANG CHANGLIN
(shoving her)
You think I don't know you steal?

INT. WANG FAMILY HOUSE - NIGHT

He Ruoqing curls on the sofa as Wang Changlin unbuckles his belt.

WANG CHANGLIN
(belt snapping)
Learn your lesson.

The blows land. He Ruoqing bites her lip, silent.

INT. UNDERGROUND CLINIC - DAY

He Ruoqing lies on a stained examination table, bleeding. The FEMALE DOCTOR (40s) shakes her head.

FEMALE DOCTOR
(to Wang Changlin)
She's lost the pregnancy. She needs rest, not more—

WANG CHANGLIN
It's just a child.

WANG MOTHER
She's young. She'll conceive again.

He Ruoqing turns her face to the wall, tears tracking down.


# === 剧本后半 ===

INT. HE FAMILY HOUSE - DAY

He Ruoqing stands before her parents, clutching her bruised arm.

HE FATHER
Divorce? Over some arguments?

HE RUOQING
He hits me. I lost the baby—

HE FATHER
(slams table)
You endure! The dowry bought your brother's future!

HE MOTHER
Ruoqing, be reasonable.

He Ruoqing's younger brother HE BAOZHEN (20s) appears, hearing the raised voices.

HE BAOZHEN
Sis, what's wrong?

HE FATHER
Nothing! She's returning to her husband.

HE BAOZHEN
(breaking)
We used your dowry for my business. It failed. We're bankrupt.

HE RUOQING
(quietly)
How much?

HE BAOZHEN
Two hundred thousand. They'll take everything.

HE FATHER
You can't leave him. We need that money.

HE BAOZHEN
(pleading)
Sis, I didn't know. I'm sorry.

He produces a kitchen knife—not threatening, but desperate.

HE BAOZHEN
I'll end this. All of it.

HE FATHER
Baozhen!

HE BAOZHEN
(pointing knife at himself)
I ruined us. Let me fix it.

HE RUOQING
Stop!

She moves toward him. He shifts, knife now toward her.

HE BAOZHEN
(to their father)
Kneel! Beg her to stay, or I cut her first!

HE MOTHER
(screaming)
Baozhen, no!

He Ruoqing's father drops to his knees.

HE FATHER
Please. For your brother.

He Ruoqing walks out, leaving the knife clattering to the floor.

EXT. STREET - DAY

He Ruoqing sits at a bus stop, trembling. She scratches a lottery ticket from her purse—last month's purchase.

The numbers match. Not a million. Fifty thousand.

She laughs, a broken sound.

INT. BEDROOM - NIGHT

Wang Changlin and Zhang Meizhen lie tangled together. His phone shows a text: BANK ALERT: 50K DEPOSIT.

WANG CHANGLIN
She actually won.

ZHANG MEIZHEN
(kissing his neck)
Take it. Say it's joint property.

WANG CHANGLIN
Divorce would only get me half. This way...

ZHANG MEIZHEN
(smiling)
She'll give it willingly. For love.

INT. BATHTUB - NIGHT

He Ruoqing soaks in a tub, red candlelight flickering. Her phone shows texts from Wang Changlin: "Forgive me. Come home."

WANG CHANGLIN and Zhang Meizhen stand over her.

HE RUOQING
(weakly)
The water... feels strange...

WANG CHANGLIN
Just relax.

He Ruoqing's eyes flutter. She tries to stand, slips. Wang Changlin pushes her shoulders down.

HE RUOQING
(struggling)
I can't... breathe...

WANG CHANGLIN
(whispering)
For our future.

Zhang Meizhen holds her legs still. He Ruoqing's hands claw at the tub's edge, then go limp.

The water stills. Only candles remain lit.
