Title: Falling for My Younger Lover
Description: A wealthy young man searches for the woman who helped him three years ago, only to find an unexpected connection that challenges their age difference.

INT. HIGHWAY - DAY

Fast-paced music plays.

JIANG HANYU, wearing racing gear and a helmet, rides a heavy motorcycle speeding down the highway. He executes a perfect drift and stops.

He removes his helmet, revealing his handsome face. A faded scar near his left temple hints at a past he doesn't advertise.

Several black sedans arrive and stop. ASSISTANT LI and a group of BODYGUARDS exit, standing respectfully in a row.

BODYGUARDS (in unison)
Welcome home, sir!

JIANG HANYU
Assistant Li. Tell me you've found her.

ASSISTANT LI
Song Yan, Longchuan TV investigative journalist. Tonight at Hanting Bar. She's working undercover on the financial piece.

JIANG HANYU
Then I'll go find her now.

ASSISTANT LI
But the old lady has arranged a matchmaking banquet—

JIANG HANYU
—is every week. This is three years coming.

He pulls his helmet back on. The engine roars as he speeds away.

EXT. STREET - THREE YEARS AGO

Flashback to three years ago.

Rain drizzles on an empty side street. YOUNG JIANG HANYU, early twenties, soaked and shivering, sits on concrete steps with a cracked phone and empty wallet beside him. His expensive watch hangs loose on his wrist.

A PASSERBY glances at him.

PASSERBY
Could call someone. Family?

JIANG HANYU (looking up, defensive)
Phone's dead. And I don't have any family left to call.

The Passerby hurries away.

SONG YAN, mid-twenties, appears under a broken streetlamp with an umbrella and takeout container. She notices his watch, his posture—someone who had everything and lost it in a day.

SONG YAN
(sitting beside him)
Pork ramen from the place around the corner. Still hot.

She slips folded bills into his jacket pocket and wraps her scarf around his neck. It's striped blue and white.

SONG YAN
(not meeting his eyes)
Just pay it forward someday.

She stands to leave. He grabs her wrist instinctively.

JIANG HANYU
I don't even know your name.

SONG YAN
(turning back, smiling faintly)
The one who should have checked the weather report.

She touches his wet hair lightly, then disappears into the rain.

JIANG HANYU (V.O.)
The teardrop mole at the corner of her eye. How did I not notice?

He touches the scarf. It's still warm.

INT. HIGHWAY - DAY

Cut back to the present.

ASSISTANT LI
She's investigating money laundering at Hanting. Undercover. Be careful she doesn't mistake you for a story.

JIANG HANYU
Then I won't give her a story. I'll give her the truth.


# === 剧本后半 ===

INT. BAR - NIGHT

Hanting Bar pulses with electronic music. SMOKE MACHINES obscure visibility.

SONG YAN, wearing a red slip dress that doesn't quite fit her style, sits at the bar with a club soda disguised as gin. A hidden camera pen clips to her clutch. She watches a group of suits in the VIP section.

SONG YAN (to herself)
Come on, Zhao. One incriminating conversation. That's all I need.

A hand reaches past her for a napkin. The wrist wears a vintage watch—familiar. She looks up.

JIANG HANYU, in a white silk shirt and black vest, meets her gaze. The scar on his temple. The confident posture. Nothing like the broken boy from the rain.

But the eyes are the same.

SONG YAN (V.O.)
Focus, Yan. Not another handsome distraction.

JIANG HANYU
Are you... waiting for someone?

SONG YAN
(sly smile)
Depends. Are you someone?

Later, in a dark corner booth. She shows him her press pass, hidden in her clutch.

SONG YAN
Promise you won't tell my editor I'm fraternizing with the target demographic.

JIANG HANYU
Your secret's safe with me.

He touches the corner of his eye instinctively. She notices the gesture, but can't place why it matters.

As they leave together, rain begins outside—just like three years ago. She shivers. He drapes his jacket over her shoulders.

The gesture stops her cold. She studies his face in the neon light. Recognition flickers.

SONG YAN
(quietly)
Have we...?

JIANG HANYU
(softly)
Not yet. But we could.

He leans in. She lets him. The kiss is soft, questioning. Not the transaction she expected.

INT. HOTEL LOBBY - NIGHT

They stumble in, drenched from the run through rain. The desk clerk raises an eyebrow. Jiang slides a credit card across the marble.

INT. HOTEL ROOM - NIGHT/MORNING

The room is modern, impersonal. Rain streaks the floor-to-ceiling windows.

On the bed, they pause. Her dress strap slides down her shoulder. His fingers trace it.

SONG YAN
(between kisses)
How old are you?

JIANG HANYU
Twenty.

She stills completely.

SONG YAN
Twenty.

(a beat)
God, when I graduated university, you were still in high school.

JIANG HANYU
Does it matter?

SONG YAN
It matters that I almost—

She pushes back, sitting up. The bedsheet falls. She's suddenly vulnerable.

SONG YAN
I have rules about this. About... everything.

JIANG HANYU
(sitting up too)
I know.

SONG YAN
(frowning)
How could you possibly—

JIANG HANYU
Because three years ago, you taught me that sometimes the people who save you don't even know they did.

He reaches for his discarded jacket, pulls out a folded, faded scarf. Blue and white stripes.

Her breath catches. She touches the corner of her eye.

SONG YAN
(whisper)
That was you. The boy in the rain.

JIANG HANYU
I'm not a boy anymore.

She looks at him—the man he's become.

SONG YAN
(stronger now)
Then prove it. Tell me your real name.

JIANG HANYU
Jiang Hanyu. I have a penthouse, a corporation, and a grandmother who wants to marry me off. But the only person I want...

He touches her cheek. The teardrop mole.

JIANG HANYU
...is the one who saw me when I was invisible.

A long beat. The rain stops outside.

SONG YAN
(standing, dressing)
Then you need to leave.

JIANG HANYU
What?

SONG YAN
Not because you're younger. Because I'm not some fantasy you built up. I'm messy. I'm a journalist who takes stupid risks. I don't do penthouses or grandmothers or...

She stops, fully dressed now. Picks up her hidden pen from the nightstand.

SONG YAN
...or men who might be the story I'm investigating.

JIANG HANYU
(realizing)
The money laundering.

SONG YAN
(sad smile)
See? You're quick. That's dangerous.

She heads for the door. He follows, still shirtless.

INT. HOTEL HALLWAY - MORNING

JIANG HANYU chases her, holding her shoes.

JIANG HANYU
Song Yan!

She stops. Turns.

JIANG HANYU
If I'm the story, investigate me. But don't run because you're scared I might be real.

He holds out her shoes.

She takes them slowly.

SONG YAN
Meet me tomorrow. Public place. You bring your financials, I'll bring my questions.

JIANG HANYU
Deal.

She walks away, but slower now. The shoes dangle from her fingers. He watches until she turns the corner, then touches the corner of his eye—where her teardrop mole would be.

The door clicks shut behind him.
